Kashi's Story

who just wants to live

Baby Kashi Dubepuria was born into the loving arms of Dr. Rohit Dubepuria and Dr. Prakruti — two devoted parents and medical doctors who dreamed of watching their daughter grow, laugh, and thrive.

At just 4 months old, her parents noticed something worrying. Kashi's cry was unusually weak. She wasn't holding her neck. Her limb movements were reduced. A quiet fear led them to AIIMS Bhopal, where Dr. Bhavna Dhingra Bhan suspected the worst and ordered a genetic test.

"We took her for tests hoping it was nothing serious. When the report came back, our world stopped. But we refuse to give up on our daughter."

— Dr. Rohit & Dr. Prakruti, Kashi's parents

As doctors themselves, Rohit and Prakruti have given their lives to helping others. Today, with humility and hope, they ask for your help to save theirs.

Understanding the Disease

Why SMA Type 1 is a medical emergency

Without treatment, SMA Type 1 causes progressive paralysis. Most children cannot sit unaided and face life-threatening breathing difficulties by age 2.

What is SMA Type 1?
A genetic disease caused by homozygous deletion of the SMN1 gene. It destroys motor neurons in the spinal cord, causing progressive muscle paralysis. SMA Type 1 is the most severe form, affecting infants.
Zolgensma — The Only Cure
Onasemnogene abeparvovec (Zolgensma) delivers a functional SMN1 gene directly to motor neurons in a single infusion. It can halt the disease entirely, giving children a chance at normal life.
Time Is Critical
Zolgensma is approved for children under 2 years and is most effective in the first months of life, before irreversible nerve damage occurs. Kashi is 5 months old — every week counts.
Cost of Treatment
Zolgensma costs approximately ₹15 crore (~$1.72 million USD) — one of the most expensive medicines in the world. It requires enormous collective effort to fund for a middle-class family.
